About

Dr. Xóchitl C. Chávez is an Assistant Professor at University of California, Riverside in the Department of Music. She is a scholar of expressive culture and performance, specializing in Indigenous communities from southern Mexico and transnational migration.

Chávez earned a Ph.D. in Cultural Anthropology with a designated emphasis in Latin American and Latino Studies from the University of California, Santa Cruz in 2013. She was a recipient of the University of California President’s Post Doctoral Fellowship (2014-2016) and a Smithsonian Institution Postdoctoral Fellow (2013-2014).

Publications

Her research has been published in various venues, including The Tide Was Always High: The Music of Latin America in Los Angeles in 2017, in Dancing Across Borders: Danzas y Bailes Mexicanos and in the Texas Hispanic Journal of Law and Policy.

Ethnographic and Documentary Film

She has also collaborated with the Smithsonian Institution as a Digital Curator and Content Specialist for the Smithsonian Latino Center Mobile Broadcast Series. Her current ethnographic documentary project, Booming Bandas of Los Ángeles focuses on second- generation Zapotec brass bands in Los Angeles County, which documenting how women and youth now fill the ranks of musicians and new leadership.

¡Booming Bandas!
at UC Riverside, 2018

Organized by Dr. Chávez

In January 2018, the Department of Music invited four indigenous Zapotec community based bands to convene outside of the Performing Arts Building for the first gathering of “Oaxacan Philharmonic Bands Audition” on any university campus in the state of California, even in the U.S.
